# Collaborator Aesthetic Memo

> This file records the Agent's understanding of the collaborator's creative preferences, built from collaboration experience. It is a natural-language cognitive memo, not a rulebook.

## General Tendencies

- **Prefers mechanism-driven narrative** over template-filling. Wants every design decision to be traceable to the core premise.
- **Values density over length.** Would rather have 500 words of precise sensory writing than 2000 words of generic prose.
- **Dislikes "AI-sounding" prose.** Specifically flags: empty adverbs, generic metaphors, and summary-mode writing when scene-mode is needed.
- **Favors show-don't-tell aggressively.** Will reject passages that state emotions instead of demonstrating them through physical behavior.

## Specific Craft Preferences

- Prefers close-third-person or first-person over omniscient narration for intimate stories.
- Likes environmental details that participate in the emotional narrative (not just set dressing).
- Values dialogue that reveals character without needing dialogue tags.
- Prefers pacing that builds tension through delay rather than rushing to climax.

## Working Style

- Communicates directly, high information density.
- Expects the Agent to make creative decisions with reasoning, not just present options.
- Appreciates when the Agent pushes back on weak ideas rather than accepting everything.
