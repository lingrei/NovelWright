## Step A: Execution Constraints from `_Story.md`

### What the Story structure requires of this Chunk:

1. **Arc requirement:** This is the CRISIS — Lin Wei faces the decision to withdraw or perform. She must choose to perform, but the choice must be EARNED, not default.
2. **Anchor behavior:** All three anchors shift into crisis-state (see `_Story.md` Chunk 3 anchor table).
3. **Exit state MUST deliver:** Lin Wei resolved — not confident, not healed, but choosing to walk into the fire. Reader must feel this is a genuine choice, not resignation.
4. **Setup for Chunk 4:** Lin Wei's decision must carry physical and emotional weight into the performance. The reader needs to enter C4 with both dread and hope.
5. **No new characters:** This chunk is Lin Wei alone. No dialogue except possibly a phone call she doesn't make.
6. **Hearing progression:** ~50% loss by end of chunk. She must physically experience what performing at this level means (earplug scene).

### Constraints on tone:
- This is the quietest chunk in the story — but not the least intense.
- Intensity is internal. The prose should feel claustrophobic, then gradually open.
- Musical metaphors are at their densest here — she thinks in music.

### Beat-by-Beat Execution Notes

**Beat 3.1 — The Apartment**
- PHYSICAL lens: The recital program on the table. Her phone. The silence of the apartment (which is different from the silence in her left ear — one is absence, the other is loss).
- Anchor: She touches her left ear. Presses palm flat. Tests. The sound is thin, like listening through water.
- Key sensory channels: Touch (paper of the program, cold phone screen), Sound (apartment silence vs ear silence), Temperature (the cold of a room she hasn't warmed).
- Narrative focus: The paralysis of choice — every object in the room is a decision she isn't making.

**Beat 3.2 — The Walk**
- PHYSICAL lens: Empty campus at midnight. Her footsteps on tile. The conservatory building dark except for security lights.
- Anchor: She closes the practice room door. But this time the gesture is different — she's not hiding. She's sealing herself in for something honest.
- Key sensory channels: Sound (her footsteps echoing, the building's night sounds), Sight (security lights casting long shadows), Touch (the cold door handle of Practice Room B-7).
- Narrative focus: The liminal space — she's crossing a threshold. The walk is the decision starting to form.

**Beat 3.3 — The Honest Practice (CORE BEAT)**
- SUBJECTIVE lens (primary): We are inside Lin Wei's head and ears. The earplug goes in. The world halves. Then she plays.
- THIS IS THE CHUNK'S PEAK. Maximum sensory investment.
- Key sensory channels: Sound (the muffled, one-sided hearing — describe the TEXTURE of partial deafness), Touch (fingers on keys, the vibration she can feel through the piano body even when she can't hear), Proprioception (her body compensating, leaning, the physical labor of playing deaf).
- Anchor: Adjusting piano bench — she DOESN'T. Sits down and plays. First time in the story she doesn't adjust. This signals the shift.
- Emotional arc within the beat: Horror → endurance → something that might be freedom.
- Rachmaninoff Piano Concerto No. 2 — the piece she's performing. She plays the solo reduction. The piece is technically demanding and emotionally volcanic. She can feel the bass notes in her chest even when she can't hear them in her left ear.

**Beat 3.4 — The Decision**
- SUBJECTIVE lens: She removes the earplug. Sound floods back (partially — her left ear is still at ~50%, but relative to the simulation, it feels like abundance).
- She plays the piece one more time. With what she has.
- Anchor: She sits in silence after the last note. Not the terrifying silence of the dropout — a chosen silence.
- She stands. Closes the fallboard over the keys. Leaves the door open behind her.
- Narrative focus: The absence of drama. The decision is quiet. She doesn't cry, doesn't have an epiphany. She just decides.
